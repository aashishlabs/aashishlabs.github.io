UPI AutoPay, ASBA & Bima-ASBA

Implementing UPI auto-collect, recurring payment, and blocked-fund mandate capabilities at NTT DATA Payment Services India.

The Context

Mandate-led UPI products extend digital payments beyond one-time collections. UPI AutoPay supports recurring e-mandates, while UPI Block/ASBA and Bima-ASBA use customer-authorized mandates to reserve funds for defined financial or insurance outcomes.

The Product Suite

  • UPI Auto-Collect & Recurring Payments: mandate registration and scheduled debit journeys for subscriptions, bills, EMIs, insurance, mutual funds, and other recurring use cases.
  • UPI Block / ASBA: customer-authorized blocking of funds with subsequent debit or release based on the underlying obligation.
  • Bima-ASBA: a one-time UPI mandate that blocks an insurance premium and permits debit when the life or health policy is issued.

The Challenge

These products require dependable orchestration across merchants, payment aggregators, banks, UPI applications, NPCI, and domain participants. Product implementation must account for mandate creation, customer authorization, status callbacks, debit or block execution, revocation, expiry, release, reconciliation, exception handling, and operational visibility.

My Role

While working with NTT DATA, I implemented and worked on UPI auto-collect and recurring payment solutions, ASBA, and Bima-ASBA. My contribution covered product implementation, mandate journeys, merchant integration requirements, API and callback flows, cross-functional coordination, testing, and operational readiness.

Outcome

Key Learning

A mandate product is a lifecycle-management system, not a single payment API. Clear state transitions, customer consent, participant ownership, callbacks, exception handling, and reconciliation determine whether the experience is trustworthy at scale.
