Neo-Banking & Payments Platform

Building and launching digital financial products across neo-banking, prepaid wallets, BBPS, and UPI in a fast-moving fintech startup.

5L+Users on the payment app
30%Improvement in product performance
3Digital products taken to market

The Context

At UDMA Technologies, I led product for a fintech startup building digital financial services for a growing user base. The objective was to move from fragmented offerings to a clearer product portfolio spanning payments, prepaid wallets, BBPS, UPI, and neo-banking.

The Challenge

The challenge was not just building an app experience. It required aligning banking partnerships, payment integrations, onboarding, compliance, operational readiness, and a viable business model in a startup environment with limited bandwidth.

My Role

As Product Head and Co-Founder, I led an eight-member cross-functional team across development, QA, business analysis, project management, UX, and growth. I worked closely with the CEO and CFO on product direction, business-model refinement, roadmap planning, external partnerships, and go-to-market execution.

Key Product Decisions

  • Prioritised BBPS, prepaid wallets, neo-banking, and UPI for the first product phase.
  • Mapped persona-led journeys to improve onboarding and feature adoption.
  • Balanced customer experience with banking-partner, compliance, and operational dependencies.
  • Used product data to identify performance bottlenecks and improve key journeys.
  • Built quarterly roadmaps to align product delivery with business priorities.

Impact

  • Launched and took three digital products to market.
  • Scaled the payment app to 5L+ users.
  • Supported growth to 1.5L+ users through persona-led feature journeys and onboarding optimisation.
  • Improved product performance by 30% through data-informed prioritisation and iteration.

Key Learning

Neo-banking is not a frontend problem. The visible app experience is only as strong as the underlying banking partnerships, payment rails, compliance controls, reconciliation processes, and operational reliability behind it.
